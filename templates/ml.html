Postseason Prediction Model

Our 2020 NCAA Tournament Predictions were built off two sets of supervised machine learning models. The first set of models were created and trained to determine which 68 out of 353 teams would make the tournament. After training and testing multiple models, including a K-Means Nearest Neighbors, Logistic Regression, Random Forest Feature Selector and a Deep Learning Neural Network, our team discovered that a Logistic Regression post Random Forest Feature Selection gave us the best accuracy and precision for predicting whether a team would make the postseason tournament or not.

Features that were deemed important according to our Random Forest included:

  • “Wins”: Number of games won

  • “Adjusted Defensive Efficiency”: An estimate of the defensive efficiency (points allowed per 100 possessions) a team would have against the average Division I offense

  • “Power Rating”: Chance of beating an average Division 1 team

  • “Wins Above Bubble.”: Referring to the cut off between making the NCAA March Madness Tournament and not making it

    • ROC Curve is reflected here, also known as the True Positive Rate. The AUC score for our Logistic Regression with the Random Forest Feature Selector was .95. This was the highest AUC score of our models. We went forward with this model as our prediction for whether a team would make the NCAA Tournament.

    Tournament Prediction Model

    As for the predictions for how well a team would fare once making the NCAA Tournament, we again trained and tested multiple models to predict how far a team would make it in the tournament. This was again supervised machine learning models including a Decision Tree, Extra-Trees Classifier, AdaBoost Classifier, K-Means Nearest Neighbors, Logistic Regression, and a Random Forest. This classification proved to be more challenging than simply determining whether a team made the tournament or not. The accuracy was closer to 53%, with precision significantly lower. The model we chose to move forward with was the Random Forest Feature Selector combined with K-Means Nearest Neighbor. The features included in the Random Forest Feature Selection were the same features as the previous model but adding “Adjusted Offensive Efficiency.” Although many models were trained and tested, it failed to give us exact predictions on the tournament, so combining predictions with precision scores we were able to come up with our best prediction for the 2020 NCAA College Basketball Tournament according to our Machine Learning Models.
